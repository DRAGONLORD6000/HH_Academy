Subject Choices

In Grade 8 and 9, the only choices that can be made are between the two (2) languages — Afrikaans FAL and IsiXhosa FAL. Students must take two (2) languages: English HL (compulsory) and one First Additional Language.


The subject choices for Grades 10 to 12 have certain restrictions which will be explained when registering. Students from Grade 10 to 12 must take seven (7) of the offered subjects, which include two (2) languages and either Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy, as well as Life Orientation.

Continuous Assessment

All assessment is based upon the National Protocol for Assessment (National Curriculum Statement). Assessment takes place frequently throughout the term in the form of Homework, Projects, Tasks, Oral work, Essays, Class tests, and more. These marks add to a learner's final promotion marks. A student absent for any assessment without a legal reason will forfeit the mark. Late submission of assignments or non-compliance will result in a nil (0) for that assessment.

A termly assessment programme will be provided to students and parents at the beginning of each term showing all dates for tests and other assessments for all subjects. Students whose academic progress is not up to standard will be retained after school for compulsory extra classes and cross-grade teaching to help them improve and get up to set standards.

Tests

Scheduled tests are written according to a set calendar supplied to all students and parents. All students from Grade 4 to 12 write these tests as part of the continuous assessment programme. A student absent on the day of a test will receive a nil (0). The calendar also stipulates dates for oral assessments and assignments.

Examinations

The Foundation Phase is assessed throughout the year according to the National Protocol for Assessment. Examinations are written in the second and fourth terms. Grade 12 students also write a Trial Examination in August/September. A Test Series is written at the end of Term 1 for Grades 4–12. Students absent during an exam must submit a relevant doctor's certificate.

Homework

All students receive homework frequently. When homework is not done, the student will receive a nil (0) as for a test. Homework that is not done on a frequent basis will cause the student to fail. Marks achieved in tests alone will not be sufficient to reach the minimum required mark to pass.

Qualifying for the Examination

Students with outstanding assessments (assignments etc.) or with school fees in arrears could be disallowed from writing School-Based Examinations (SBA). This is also valid for the Test Series written at the end of the first term. Parents are requested to assist their children in planning their studies and managing their time. Late submission of assignments will be penalised.

Reports

Reports are handed out at the end of each term. The second term report will be handed out at the beginning of the third term. No report will be handed out to students with outstanding school fees. An ACADEMIC WRITTEN WARNING will accompany all reports of students in danger of failing.

Extra Classes

All staff members offer extra classes in the afternoon if required. Students and parents are invited to discuss this with individual educators if the need arises. Grade 12 students also have the opportunity to attend a winter school during the July break. We also offer Cross Grade teaching in the afternoon and Saturday School. Students can also make use of classes offered at our Saturday School.
