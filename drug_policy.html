Happy Home Academy Drugs Policy

This policy is here to explain the academy's expectations, prevention approach, and intervention process around alcohol, tobacco, illicit drugs, prescription medication, inhalants, and substance abuse.

Happy Home Academy's

Policy Approach

Prevention

The academy encourages a culture of drug avoidance both at school and at home. This includes education about drug and substance abuse starting in primary school and continuing throughout education according to each learner's developmental stage. Education involves acquiring knowledge about the potential harm of drugs, encouragement to make informed choices, and developing strategies to avoid using them. Our care education program emphasizes well-being, self-esteem, and holistic development—spiritually, intellectually, physically, and emotionally.

Expectations

Within the Christian tradition, each person is called to realize and live the dignity that comes with being human. Fundamental to living our dignity is caring for our body. The school's expectations are derived from a deep concern for student well-being and a holistic approach to development. Students will not abuse drugs or other substances at school, during school functions, or at school-organized activities. This policy includes smoking, illicit drugs, alcohol, the misuse of prescribed medicines, and other substances. The academy cooperates with parents in discouraging use at all times.

Intervention

A school response to drug use involves transparent processes: informing parents and relevant agencies, and using interventions that assist rehabilitation. The academy maintains a clear policy of working with parents and caregivers with the common objective of guarding children from harmful drug use or substance abuse. Consequences are concerned with supporting the learner while maintaining school standards, and helping them develop resilience and wise decision-making.

Introduction

The use and abuse of alcohol, cigarettes, other substances such as performance enhancing drugs, prescription drugs when not taken according to a medical practitioner's prescribed regime, inhalants, and illicit drugs are prevalent amongst adolescents in South Africa.

Happy Home Academy, in common with other schools, has a responsibility to address the problem of drug or substance abuse, both within the school environment and outside school hours.

The Happy Home Academy Drugs Policy aims to educate students about informed personal choices that promote personal well-being and maximize learning opportunities. The school has developed a policy regarding drugs and young people that is designed to discourage students from using drugs and, for those who are already doing so, to prevent them from becoming drug dependent.

Prevention

Providing information and strategies that assist in preventing drug and substance use and misuse.

Expectations

Clearly outlining the academy's expectations regarding illicit drugs, alcohol, tobacco, prescription medication, and other substances.

Intervention

Identifying the processes that will be followed if a student is found to be in breach of this policy.

What Is Drug and Substance Abuse?

Drug and substance abuse involves the use of alcohol or tobacco by people under the age of 18 years, the use of illicit drugs, and the misuse of medications and other substances.

Prevention

Happy Home Academy encourages a culture of drug avoidance both at school and at home. This is achieved through several initiatives.

A firm and clear statement on the abuse of drugs and other substances. The school's expectation is that students will not abuse drugs or other substances at school or school functions, and the Academy will cooperate with parents in discouraging use at all times. This policy includes smoking, illicit drugs, alcohol, the misuse of prescribed medicines, and other substances.

Drug education. Education about drug and substance abuse is essential. This must start in primary school and continue throughout education according to each learner's developmental stage. Education involves the acquisition of knowledge about the potential harm of drugs, encouragement to make informed choices on drugs, and the development of strategies to avoid using them.

For a number of years, Happy Home Academy has offered an educational programme designed to make students aware of the benefits of well-being, and to give them the knowledge and confidence to make wise choices about their lifestyles. A significant part of this programme is related to issues involving alcohol, tobacco, pain relievers, and illicit drugs.

The care education programme is closely linked with Happy Home Academy's overall philosophy, which emphasizes the development of a sense of value and the promotion of self-esteem in children and adolescents. This is designed to help them develop spiritually, intellectually, physically, and emotionally. Supporting this, and acting as a deterrent, is the school's intervention process with regard to drug use.

A school response to the use of drugs includes consequences concerned with transparent processes: informing parents and relevant agencies, and using interventions that assist rehabilitation.

The Academy maintains a clear policy of working with parents and caregivers, with the common objective of guarding children from harmful drug use or substance abuse.

Expectations

Within the Christian tradition, each person is called to realize and live the dignity that comes with being human. Fundamental to living our dignity is caring for our body. Caring for our body is a physical, intellectual, spiritual, and moral activity.

The school's expectation regarding drug use is derived from a deep concern for, and a holistic approach to, student well-being. Drug and substance use and abuse compromises well-being and exhibits a failure to recognize our dignity as human beings. Therefore, this policy makes clear the school's expectations of students within Happy Home Academy, at Happy Home Academy functions, and at activities organized by Happy Home Academy.

Alcohol

The school's policy is that students may not consume alcohol in the school, at any school function, or at any activity organized by the school in any circumstance or in any company.

Tobacco

Students may not smoke at school, while transiting to or from school, while wearing the school uniform, while representing the school, or while attending school functions, unless otherwise indicated to be permitted.

Illicit Drugs

Students may not use any illegal substance at school, to or from school, while wearing the school uniform, at any Happy Home Academy function, or at any Happy Home Academy organized activity. Students who traffic illegal drugs will be subject to a more formal follow-up process involving police and parents or caregivers.

Prescription Medication

Students who need to take prescribed medication while at school must adhere to their doctor's recommended dosage and must not allow another student access to these drugs.

The student's name and dosage requirements for prescription medication must be clearly visible. Medication will be stored in a locked cabinet. The First Aid Officer will keep a record of the dosage, time, and date of administration of medication.

Prescription Medication for GET Learners

  • Must have a note in their diary from a parent, caregiver, or legal guardian notifying the school of the need to use medication.
  • Must surrender medication to the First Aid Officer at the Student Services Office, unless this would inhibit a student's safety and well-being.
  • Must have medication administered in the Student Services Office.
  • Must notify their teacher when they are going to take medication.
  • May take prescribed medication while at school according to the approved process.

Year FET Learners

  • Must have a note in their diary from a parent, caregiver, or legal guardian notifying the school of the need to use medication.
  • Are asked to surrender medication to the First Aid Officer at the Student Services Office, unless this would inhibit a student's safety and well-being.
  • Are recommended to administer medication in the Student Services Office.

Inhalants and Possession of Drugs

Inhalants

Students are not permitted to inhale solvents, volatile substances, or any other substance that alters their conscious state.

Possession of Drugs

Students are not permitted to possess drugs, except prescribed medication, at school, while transiting to or from school, while wearing the school uniform, at any Happy Home Academy function, or at any Happy Home Academy organized activity.
