CODE OF CONDUCT

Discipline & Dedication

Happy Home Academy is driven by sound moral principles, academic excellence and passionate participation. Our Code of Conduct promotes an atmosphere of co-operation, mutual respect and a culture of tolerance and peace. It applies to all learners wearing the HHA school uniform or officially representing the school.

Our Foundation

Mission, Ethos & Language

Mission Statement

Happy Home Academy is committed to providing facilities for a well-balanced education — concentrating on the development of mind, body and soul — enabling every child to develop their full potential and play a useful, fulfilling role in the community and larger society.

Our Ethos

We are driven by sound moral principles, academic excellence and passionate participation. Our Code of Conduct recognises the need to promote co-operation, teaching, learning, mutual respect and the establishment of a culture of tolerance and peace.

Language Policy

The medium of instruction at HH Academy is English. Afrikaans and isiXhosa are offered as first additional languages.

Rights & Responsibilities

Learners & Parents

Learner Rights

  • Equality
  • Dignity
  • Protection from all forms of discrimination
  • A safe environment conducive to learning

Learner Responsibilities

  • Have respect for yourself

School Rules

Policies & Procedures

These rules are non-negotiable and were developed in consultation with staff and the School Governing Body.

  • Anti-social behaviour will not be tolerated
  • Cell phones or similar devices must be switched off and not visible during the school day
  • No talking out of turn
  • No abusive language
  • No vandalism or littering
  • No walking around without permission and a pass
  • No involvement in activities unrelated to the subject being taught without the teacher's consent
  • Be punctual for class
  • Homework tasks and missed work due to absence must be completed timeously
  • Homework must be completed at home, not before school
COVID-19 Rules
  • Learners must always have their masks on
  • Maintain 1.5m physical distance at all times
  • Sharing of items including stationery is prohibited
  • Physical contact (handshakes, hugging) is prohibited

Transgression of any of the above rules will have consequences.

  • The register teacher will keep an accurate attendance register and report irregular attendance to parents and HODs
  • Any absence must be covered by an absentee note from a parent
  • Absences longer than 3 days require a doctor's certificate
  • After 10 consecutive unexplained days absent, the learner's record may be cancelled and re-application is required (at the Principal's discretion)
  • After 20 unexplained days absent in total for the year, the learner will be deregistered
  • All learners must arrive before the official starting time; late arrivals are recorded in the entrance register
  • Absence from a formal examination must be notified by the parent and supported by a doctor's certificate
  • Absence due to COVID-19 symptoms: refer to the Covid-19 management policy
Early Release
  • No learner may leave during school hours without a parental letter; the learner must obtain a "permission to go home" note signed by the class teacher
  • If a learner falls ill, the class teacher signs a permission note and the administrator contacts the parent to arrange transport
  • A learner may NOT use a cell phone to contact a parent directly without the administrator's permission
Religious or Cultural Practices
  • Learners wishing to participate in religious or cultural ceremonies may do so once parents have given the Principal due notice

Every learner is obligated to protect and carefully use all school facilities and equipment. Destruction or vandalism is a punishable offence. Parents or legal guardians of anyone who intentionally misuses or damages school property will replace or pay for the damaged property. CCTV cameras are installed for the protection and safety of all staff and learners.

Out-of-Bounds Areas
  • The area behind the Director's office (unless doing sports)
  • The parking area at the lakeside
  • The area on the left side of the security room
  • The Lake
  • Home Affairs premises

Lost property found on premises will be taken to the administrator's office for collection.

  • The school is not responsible for cell phones, tablets or electronic equipment lost or stolen at school or during school-related activities
  • If brought to school, cell phones must be switched off from the moment the learner enters the premises until the end-of-day bell, and stored at the administrator's office
  • 1st confiscation: held for 1 week · 2nd confiscation (same term): held for 2 weeks · 3rd confiscation: held for a month
  • Learners may not use cell phones at school
  • Personal computer games, toys, iPods, laptops and similar devices may not be brought to school without prior arrangement with a teacher
Cash Payments
  • Any fees paid by a learner on behalf of a parent must be paid at the Bursar's Office before the school day starts
  • Learners are discouraged from bringing large sums of money to school
  • Parents are encouraged to pay school fees via Debit Order, EFT, Direct Deposit, or card at the Bursar's Office
  • Badges: Only badges received at HHA are permitted
  • Jewellery & Body Piercing: No jewellery of any kind is allowed — including studs, earrings, chains, rings, bracelets, tongue rings, lip studs or eyebrow studs of any material. Medic-Alert bracelets are the only exception
  • Make-up: Not allowed at any time, including Civvies days. Learners arriving with make-up will be asked to remove it immediately
  • Nails: Must be kept short, clean and neat. No nail varnish — learners found wearing nail polish will be sent to the administrator (nail polish remover available for R5.00)
  • Tattoos: Must not be visible when wearing school uniform or sports uniform
  • General: Only a plain white vest (not visible above the collar) may be worn under the shirt. No T-shirts under a summer shirt. On Civvies days, learners must wear decent clothes — no short dresses, skirts or bum shorts for girls

Process

Disciplinary Procedures & Hearings

The following measures are aimed at correcting behaviour before suspension and, in extreme circumstances, expulsion:

  • Verbal warning / reprimand
  • Use of the de-merit diary system
  • Written warning / final written warning
  • Removal from classes to the exclusion zone
  • Written punishment; community service
  • Homework detention / supervised detention / de-merit detention
  • Referral for counselling
  • Compensation for damages (repair or replacement of lost or damaged items)
  • Temporary suspension of school-related privileges
  • Exclusion from school activities (academic dinner, sporting events, valedictory, prize-giving)
  • Withdrawal of recognition (award withdrawn, councillor badge rescinded)
  • Temporary suspension from class or school — pending a disciplinary hearing
  • Formal disciplinary hearing

A learner will be guilty of serious misconduct leading to potential suspension if they intentionally and without just excuse:

  • Seriously threaten, disrupt or frustrate teaching or learning in a class
  • Engage in a conspiracy to disrupt the proper functioning of the school through collective action
  • Insult the dignity of or defame any learner or person (including racist remarks)
  • Distribute or possess test or examination material that may enable unfair advantage
  • Cheat in a test, examination or any other form of assessment
  • Engage in any act of public indecency
  • Sexually harass another person
  • Possess or distribute pornographic material
  • Are under the influence of, or in possession of, alcohol or drugs

A learner faces expulsion if they:

  • Are found guilty of the same or similar misconduct after a prior finding of guilt
  • Fail to comply with a suspension as a correctional measure
  • Forge any document or signature to the prejudice of the school
  • Trade in any test or examination question paper
  • Engage in theft or act dishonestly to the prejudice of another person
  • Possess, consume or deal in any illegal substance
  • Are in possession of a dangerous weapon
  • Assault or threaten to assault another person
  • Hold any person hostage
  • Murder any person
  • Rape any person or engage in any sexual activity that constitutes an offence
  • Maliciously damage property

The Principal and staff will make every effort to correct the situation before resorting to a full hearing. Full hearings include members of the Student Council and Governing Body.

  • For serious or continuous misdemeanours, the learner will be required to attend a full hearing
  • For lesser misdemeanours, at the Principal and Discipline Officer's discretion, the parent(s) or guardian will be called to a meeting to discuss the misbehaviour
  • Any matters not specifically addressed in this Code will be dealt with by the Principal in line with the school's ethos and the laws of the country
